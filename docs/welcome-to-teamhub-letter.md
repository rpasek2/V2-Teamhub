## What is TeamHub?

TeamHub is where everything about your gymnast's team experience lives in one place. Instead of juggling group chats, spreadsheets, and multiple apps, TeamHub gives you one spot to:

- See your gymnast's scores, skills, attendance, and assignments
- Get messages and announcements from coaches
- View the team calendar and RSVP to events
- Track competitions and meet results
- And much more

There are two ways to use TeamHub: the **website** and the **mobile app**. You'll want to set up both.

## Step 1: Create Your Account on the Website

You need to create your account on the website first. The mobile app uses the same login.

1. On your computer or phone browser, go to **gymteamhub.com**
2. Click **Create Account**
3. **Use the email address listed as your Primary email in iClassPro.** This is how we link you to your gymnast(s). If you're not sure which email that is, check your iClassPro account or ask me.
4. Choose a password and complete the signup
5. You'll be asked to enter an **invite code** to join your gym's hub — I'll provide this separately. **You must join the hub on the website first.** The mobile app's join hub feature is not working yet, so make sure to complete this step on the website before logging into the app.

**Important:** If you want to use a different email than what's in iClassPro, message me first so I can update it before you sign up. If you sign up with the wrong email, your gymnast won't be linked to your account.

**Adding a second parent:** Have the first parent sign up and join the hub first. Then message me on TeamHub and I'll link the second parent to your gymnast's profile.

## Step 2: Set Up Your Account

Once you're logged in on the website, do these things right away:

1. **Find your gymnast** — Click **Roster** in the sidebar on the left. Your gymnast should appear with their name and level. Click their name to open their profile. If your gymnast is missing or shows incorrect info, message me.

2. **Set your privacy** — Click the **gear icon** at the bottom of the sidebar to open **Settings**. Scroll to **Privacy Settings**. Here you choose whether other parents can see your email, phone number, your gymnast's level, and your gymnast's birthday. Everything is hidden by default except level.

3. **Update your gymnast's info** — From your gymnast's profile (in Roster), you can update their apparel sizes, medical info, and emergency contacts.

4. **Send a test message** — Click **Messages** in the sidebar. You can send a direct message to any coach or staff member. Try sending one to make sure it works!

## What You Can Do on the Website

The website (gymteamhub.com) is the full-featured experience. You navigate using the **sidebar on the left side of the screen**. Here's what each section does:

- **Dashboard** — Your home screen. Shows your linked gymnasts, upcoming events, and recent announcements at a glance.
- **Roster** — View your gymnast's profile with their level, medical info, emergency contacts, and floor music. You can edit your gymnast's info here.
- **Calendar** — See all gym events and birthdays. You can RSVP to events (going / maybe / not going).
- **Messages** — Send direct messages to coaches and staff in real time. You can also have group conversations in team channels.
- **Groups** — Your team's shared space. View posts from coaches, vote in polls, sign up for volunteer slots, and RSVP to group events.
- **Competitions** — See upcoming and past meets, session times, and which gymnasts are competing.
- **Scores** — View your gymnast's meet scores across the season with charts, averages, season bests, and qualifying score progress.
- **Skills** — Track your gymnast's skill progression broken down by event (vault, bars, beam, floor).
- **Assignments** — See practice assignments that your gymnast's coach has set.
- **Attendance** — Check your gymnast's attendance record.
- **Progress Reports** — Read reports from coaches about your gymnast's development.
- **Marketplace** — Buy and sell used leotards, grips, and other gear with other gym families.
- **Resources** — Access documents and files shared by the gym.
- **Announcements** — Important gym-wide announcements pop up when you log in and also show on your dashboard.

## What You Can Do on the Mobile App

The mobile app has most of the same features as the website, designed for quick access on your phone. You navigate using the **tabs at the bottom of the screen**.

The mobile app includes a few extras you won't find on the website:

- **Push notifications** — Get instant alerts on your phone when you receive a message, announcement, or update. You can customize exactly which features send you notifications by going to **Settings > Notification Preferences**. From there, you can toggle notifications on or off for each feature individually — Messages, Groups, Calendar, Competitions, Scores, Skills, Assignments, Marketplace, and Resources. For example, if you only want to be notified about messages and scores, you can turn off everything else.
- **Floor music offline** — Download your gymnast's floor music so you can play it at competitions even without internet
- **Customizable navigation** — Choose which tabs appear in your bottom bar (go to Settings > Customize Tabs)
- **Dark mode** — Full dark mode support (toggle in Settings)

### What's different between the website and the app?

Both the website and the mobile app connect to the same account and the same data. A message you send on the website shows up on the app, and vice versa. Your gymnast's scores, skills, and everything else are the same in both places.

The main differences:

- **Navigation** — On the website you use the sidebar on the left. On the app you use the tabs at the bottom.
- **Creating posts** — You can create group posts on the website. The app doesn't support creating posts yet (but you can read and interact with them).
- **Marketplace** — On the website you can create, edit, and browse listings. On the app you can browse only.
- **Score charts** — The website has detailed charts and metrics. The app has a simplified view.
- **Push notifications** — Only available on the mobile app. The website does not send push notifications to your phone.
- **Floor music offline** — Only available on the mobile app. You can download your gymnast's floor music for playback without internet.

**You don't need to use both**, but I recommend it. Use the website when you're on a computer and want to dig into details or create posts. Use the app when you want to quickly check a score, read a message, or play floor music at a meet.

## Reporting Bugs & Suggesting Features

Your feedback is critical — this is how TeamHub gets better. Here's exactly how to report something:

1. Go to **Settings** (gear icon in the sidebar on web, or the Settings tab on mobile)
2. Scroll down to **Report a Bug / Suggest a Feature**
3. Choose either **Bug Report** or **Feature Request** using the toggle
4. Fill in a **title** (a short description of the issue) and **details** (what happened, what you expected, what screen you were on)
5. Tap **Submit**

**Tips for a helpful bug report:**
- What page or screen were you on?
- What did you tap or click?
- What happened vs. what you expected to happen?
- Does it happen every time, or did it only happen once?
- A screenshot helps a lot if you can take one!

You can also just message me directly on TeamHub — I check messages frequently and that's totally fine.

## What to Look For While Testing

I need your help finding anything that doesn't work or doesn't feel right:

1. **Does anything crash or show an error?** — Note what screen you were on and what you tapped
2. **Is any text cut off, overlapping, or hard to read?**
3. **Does your gymnast show up correctly?** — Right name, right level, right info?
4. **Are scores and attendance accurate?** — Compare with what you know from meets
5. **Do messages work?** — Send a test message to a coach and confirm they get it
6. **Do push notifications work?** — On mobile, check that you get a notification when someone messages you
7. **Is anything confusing?** — If you can't figure out how to do something, that's a problem I need to fix
8. **What's missing?** — Is there something you need that you can't find?
