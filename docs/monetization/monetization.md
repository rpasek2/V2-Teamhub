# GymTeamHub: Business & Monetization Strategy

## 1. Corporate & Financial Infrastructure
To ensure liability protection and smooth revenue flow for Two Trees Software LLC, personal and business finances must be strictly separated.

* **Banking:** Acquire an EIN and open a dedicated business checking account (e.g., Novo, Mercury, Chase Business).
* **Payment Processor:** Use **Stripe**. It handles recurring subscriptions, prorations, and securely routes funds to the business account.
* **Tax Compliance:** Use **Stripe Tax Basic** (monitoring-only tier). It costs $0 at launch because we have not crossed the "Economic Nexus" threshold (usually $100k or 200 transactions) in any other state. Illinois does not charge state sales tax on SaaS. Stripe will automatically monitor our sales and alert us when/if we cross a threshold in another state.

## 2. Technical Implementation
* **Web Dashboard:** The desktop web app is the administrative command center. All Hub creation, subscription upgrades, and Stripe Checkout flows happen exclusively here.
* **Supabase & Webhooks:** When a user pays on the web via Stripe, a Stripe Webhook updates their role/permissions in the Supabase database to unlock premium features.
* **Video Storage:** To keep server costs low, GymTeamHub does not natively host video. Coaches utilize the "Links/Docs" tab to share external, zero-cost URLs (like Google Photos albums or unlisted YouTube videos).
* **Operating Costs:** Expect ~$25–$30/month (Supabase Pro plan for automated backups + Firebase Hosting).

## 3. The Mobile App Strategy (Bypassing App Store Fees)
To avoid the 15%–30% Apple/Google App Store tax, the mobile app operates strictly as a "Companion App" for coaches, parents, and athletes.

* **No Paywalls on Mobile:** There are no "Upgrade" buttons or links to Stripe on the mobile app. 
* **No Hub Creation on Mobile:** The "Create a Hub" button is removed from mobile builds entirely. 
* **Frictionless Onboarding:** Parents and athletes can download the app and create a free account. They join their specific team by entering an **Invite Code** generated by the Head Coach on the web dashboard.
* **App Store Review:** Provide Apple/Google reviewers with a dummy account and an active Invite Code in the review notes. The app functions perfectly as a read-only companion utility, ensuring compliance with their anti-steering rules.

## 4. Go-to-Market & Pricing Rollout
A phased approach to build a loyal user base, gather feedback, and eventually scale revenue.

### Phase 1: Local Beta ("Design Partners")
* **The Offer:** Free lifetime access for the first ~5 local gyms (personal coaching network).
* **The Catch:** They pay in rigorous feedback. 
* **White-Glove Onboarding:** We manually input their DP rosters and schedules to remove all friction and ensure immediate usage.
* **Goal:** Generate concrete testimonials and case studies.

### Phase 2: Public Launch ("Founding Members")
* **The Offer:** A heavily discounted "All-Access" tier (e.g., $59–$79/month).
* **Goal:** Create urgency for the first 10-20 paying gyms, locking them in at a grandfathered rate in exchange for early adoption.

### Phase 3: Standard Pricing (Tiered)
Once the app is proven at scale, transition to standard B2B pricing for all new sign-ups.
* **Basic Tier (~$49/mo):** Core roster, calendar, and basic communication.
* **Pro Tier (~$79/mo):** Core + Advanced Analytics (scores, Start Values, DP requirements).
* **Elite Tier (~$99 - $119+/mo):** All features unlocked, including travel logistics, apparel sizing, etc.

## 5. Long-Term Goal: The Micro-SaaS Exit
The ultimate objective is to build a highly profitable, low-churn asset that becomes an attractive acquisition target.

* **The Target Buyer:** Larger "All-in-One" gym management platforms (iClassPro, Jackrabbit), sports tech aggregators (TeamSnap), or Micro-Private Equity firms.
* **The Positioning:** Do not build class billing. Stay hyper-focused on solving the complex edge cases of competitive DP gymnastics. Become the missing, high-value puzzle piece that a larger incumbent would rather buy than build from scratch.
